Reviora — AI Operations Copilot

An in-house AI chatbot that consolidated fragmented process documentation and eliminated hours of daily operational inefficiency — reducing knowledge retrieval overhead by 95%.

95% Efficiency gain
44 hrs → 3 hrs Monthly overhead
5–6 days Recovered per month
NotebookLMPrompt EngineeringKnowledge ManagementOps Design

The problem

Process documentation was deeply fragmented — multiple versions of the same process existed simultaneously, with no clear source of truth. Every time a process was updated, previous iterations lingered across drives and inboxes, creating confusion.

Beyond documentation, the team spent significant time resolving process-related queries through repeated communication. A measured baseline of approximately 2 hours per day — 44 hours per month, or 5–6 working days — was being lost to documentation search and repetitive clarifications.

What was built

Reviora is an in-house AI operations copilot built on NotebookLM, designed to consolidate the team's entire knowledge base and surface it conversationally. It acts as an always-available resource for process queries, onboarding, cross-team understanding, and operational best practices.

The build involved three phases: processes were categorized into structured domains — Operational, Process-Related, Team-Related, Onboarding, and others. Every process, update, team member detail, and skill matrix entry was then mapped out in full. Finally — and critically — each document was written with AI readability in mind, optimizing structure and language for how a language model would consume and reason over the content.

Key capabilities

  • Instant resolution of process and operational queries without escalation
  • Automated drafting of vendor query responses based on documented processes
  • Team-level visibility via skill matrices — enabling managers to match tasks to the right individuals
  • Cross-team collaboration: different teams could independently understand each other's processes without repeated back-and-forth
  • Standardized onboarding experience for new team members

Outcome

Impact was measured over one month of post-implementation tracking. The reduction was consistent across the team, not just at the individual level.

Spam & Fraud Detection Model

A hybrid detection system combining SQL workflows, formula-based automation, and AI-assisted prompts to identify spam, fraud, and information discrepancies — lifting accuracy from 72% to 92%.

72% → 92% Detection accuracy
75%+ Efficiency gain
Eliminated Manual detection effort
SQLSpreadsheet AutomationAI PromptingFraud Detection

The problem

Spam and fraud detection was done entirely by hand. Analysts manually reviewed records for signs of fraud, spam patterns, and information discrepancies — a process prone to oversight, inconsistent judgment, and human error.

The manual approach had a measurable ceiling: detection accuracy sat between 72–78%, meaning a significant proportion of fraud and spam was either missed or incorrectly actioned. There was no systematic pattern recognition in place.

What was built

A multi-layer detection system that operates from a single raw data input source. The system runs records through an extensive formula-based spreadsheet template and SQL workflows that automatically surface records with high fraud or spam probability. AI-assisted prompts layer on top to catch patterns that rule-based logic alone may miss.

Detected records are categorized automatically based on the pattern type exhibited — spam, fraud, or information discrepancy — giving analysts a pre-triaged queue rather than an undifferentiated list to review manually.

Key capabilities

  • Single-input automation — raw data in, categorized output out
  • Pattern-based classification across spam, fraud, and discrepancy types
  • SQL workflow integration for scalable data processing
  • AI-assisted prompt layer for nuanced pattern identification beyond rule-based logic
  • Elimination of manual review as the primary detection mechanism

AI Persona Suite — Team-Scale Automation

A suite of six purpose-built AI personas deployed team-wide to standardize analysis, documentation, and irregularity detection — replacing manual, inconsistent, and error-prone processes.

6 Personas built
Team-wide Adoption
Standardized Analysis & docs
Prompt EngineeringAI PersonasWorkflow AutomationStandardization

The problem

A range of recurring operational tasks — deep-dive analysis, documentation, irregularity identification — were being handled manually, individually, and without standardization. Output quality varied by person, analysis was time-intensive, and processes susceptible to human error in judgment had no systematic checks in place.

What was built

A suite of six AI personas, each purpose-built for a specific operational need and deployed team-wide. Each persona was designed with extensive prompt instructions, calibrated against real organizational context, and built to operate consistently regardless of who is using it.

Personas built

  • Documentation Assistant — generates formal, standardized proposals and process documents using organization-wide documents as reference, producing consistent output regardless of who initiates it
  • Analysis Assistant — accepts raw data input and conducts deep strategic analysis, surfacing business insights and identifying problem areas; replaced an entirely manual, unstandardized process
  • Irregularities Identification Assistant — scans records for patterns including word count anomalies, harsh content, and Conflict of Interest flags; eliminates reliance on individual judgment for detection
  • Three additional personas covering other recurring operational workflows

Outcome

All six personas were adopted at the team level, standardizing output quality across analysis, documentation, and compliance-related tasks. Processes previously dependent on individual effort and judgment became consistent, repeatable, and significantly faster — particularly deep-dive analysis, which had no prior standardization at all.

TAT Intelligence Tool — SLA Breach & Performance System

A two-layer operational system that detects SLA breaches in real time and surfaces records for priority processing, while tracking team efficiency over time — reducing daily reporting overhead by 93%.

15 min → <1 min Daily reporting time
Automated Priority queue surfacing
Real-time Team efficiency tracking
Spreadsheet AutomationSLA ManagementPerformance Analytics

The problem

TAT management was handled entirely manually. Daily reporting took around 15 minutes each day. There was no systematic process for surfacing records breaching the defined TAT — identification relied on manual Excel filtering. And there was no performance intelligence layer at all: team efficiency against TAT was not being measured in any structured way.

What was built

A two-layer system built on formula-based spreadsheet automation. The first layer handles operational prioritization: it automatically identifies records breaching defined TAT thresholds and surfaces them directly to the team for priority processing — replacing manual Excel filtering entirely.

The second layer handles performance intelligence: it tracks the TAT of processed records over time, enabling the team to measure actual efficiency, identify bottlenecks, and manage turnaround proactively. This layer did not exist before this build.

Key capabilities

  • Real-time identification of records breaching defined TAT thresholds
  • Automatic surfacing of breach records for priority processing
  • Team-level efficiency measurement through processed record TAT tracking
  • Gap and bottleneck identification at the performance level
  • Daily reporting reduced from 15 minutes to under 1 minute

Automation · Standardization

Automated Note Generator

A formula-driven template that automatically analyzes reviewer-level entities from raw data and produces standardized notes — cutting processing time by 93% while improving consistency across the team.

93% Time reduction
15 min → <1 min Per record
Standardized Team-wide notes
Spreadsheet AutomationFormula EngineeringProcess Design

The problem

Notes for reviewer-level records were written manually — analysts reviewed each entity individually and composed notes from scratch, taking around 15 minutes per record. At volume, this was a significant drag. Beyond time, there was a standardization problem: notes varied in structure and language across the team, making tracking and management of records harder than it needed to be.

What was built

A formula-driven spreadsheet template that accepts raw data as input and automatically analyzes all entities of the reviewer. Based on that analysis, a structured note is generated automatically — no manual composition required. The template was standardized and deployed across the team, replacing individual variation with a consistent output format.
